The link won't open here...

but there are two kind of 'voxel renderers'. One is the classic demo scene landscape effect that I recreated, and another is the literal meaning of voxels (volume pixels), where an object is built out of tiny colored cubes and rendered that way.
Maybe you're confusing those.

The name for my effect is not completely wrong though. Originally, you'd have a grayscale bitmap that depicted height. You could imagine a landscape being built from this by converting every pixel into a vertical bar of a certain height. Every bar would be a volume-equivalent of a pixel, so also a voxel. Remember, 'pixel' stands for 'picture element', so 'voxel' is 'volume element'. The bars in the classic effect are just as much volume-elements as the 'real' cubic voxels.

Mine doesn't use a bitmap for a heightfield, but rather a function in 2 variables. But the rendering method is still the same as the old way (stepping over the landscape and comparing the projected height of the last step to the current).
