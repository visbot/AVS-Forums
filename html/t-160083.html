Yum - Yummy indeed. Very nice color scheme. Great superscope-created texture. The TV theme works very well here. Excellent choice for an intro.

Rainbow Ball - You're right, it does look organic. Sweet colors, like oil in water or something.

Goolicious - Extremely impressive technical preset. The DM looks hella-complicated. Colors aren't bad either.

License to Chill - Hahaha, what a cheesy name. Sweet as hell preset though. That dancer is really convincing. Awesome work.

Insane Architect - AAH too much! I liked the origional better, but the colors aren't bad on this. The proplem is that even soft music makes the scope go fucking insane!

Seismogrid - Possibly my favorite preset ever. Texer and scopes go together PERFECTLY. Amazing wavelike movement. Colors add so much to this movement. You got this effect nailed.

VJ Chmutov - Amazing. I love how you make this DM look like a solid shape. The camera moves perfectly too. The red tone works excellently too. It seems to slow down in framerate when the camera isn't pointed at the opject though. Odd. Still sweet as fuck though.

Hello Light - Very cool. The colors aren't perfect though. I see what kind of scheme you were going for with the aqua and red, but it could use a bit of tweaking. I prefer the original, but this isn't bad either.

Silk Strings - This looks so effortless (and yet I know it's actually a product of hours or work). Perfect colors, amazingly smooth movement. I love it.

Bipolar - This is cool. A nicely lit, colorfully blurred preset. A little too fast for me though.

Tokyo Bullet - Sweet! This is a huge improvement over the original. Very creative signs and whatnot. Great, smooth movement. So much work went into this and it SHOWS!

Elevation - Ha, thanks for mentioning me! I know I've said this before, but great preset. This is what I had in mind when making Claustrophobia.

Don't Trip and Drive - Normally I wouldn't like these outrageous colors. But considering the fact that this whole preset is outrageous, I like them a lot! Great interactivity, and awesome technical work.

Around the World - This is a great way to end the pack. The way the flags are created is awesome. The patterns are perfect. Great wavy movement, as usual for you. The BG actually adds a lot to this preset too. Great choice of coloring for it.

The link won't open here...

but there are two kind of 'voxel renderers'. One is the classic demo scene landscape effect that I recreated, and another is the literal meaning of voxels (volume pixels), where an object is built out of tiny colored cubes and rendered that way.
Maybe you're confusing those.

The name for my effect is not completely wrong though. Originally, you'd have a grayscale bitmap that depicted height. You could imagine a landscape being built from this by converting every pixel into a vertical bar of a certain height. Every bar would be a volume-equivalent of a pixel, so also a voxel. Remember, 'pixel' stands for 'picture element', so 'voxel' is 'volume element'. The bars in the classic effect are just as much volume-elements as the 'real' cubic voxels.

Mine doesn't use a bitmap for a heightfield, but rather a function in 2 variables. But the rendering method is still the same as the old way (stepping over the landscape and comparing the projected height of the last step to the current).
